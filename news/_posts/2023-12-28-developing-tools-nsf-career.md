*Closing remarks for the NSF CAREER award IIS-1845836*

*Originally published on [Memming's Substack](https://memming.substack.com/p/developing-tools-to-uncover-the-collective)*

---

This post documents the conclusion of an NSF CAREER award (IIS-1845836, 2019-2023) focused on understanding brain dynamics through neural data analysis. Our research took a bottom-up approach, letting the measured neural activity tell us what the underlying algorithm was in the language of nonlinear dynamical systems.

## Research Aims

### Aim 1: Interpretable Neural Modeling

We advanced theoretical understanding by showing that periodic and quasi-periodic attractors can also support learning arbitrarily long temporal relationships. A significant finding involved the Hida-Matern connection, demonstrating that widely used Gaussian process priors on neural trajectories are equivalent to a latent linear stochastic process, which limits their ability to capture nonlinear dynamics.

### Aim 2: Real-Time Model Fitting

The team developed methods for faster neural signal inference, including linear-time Gaussian process approaches and improved Bayesian filtering techniques to enable real-time neural state-space manipulation. These advances allow for real-time brain state monitoring and manipulation.

### Aim 3: Brain State Control

This aim sought to develop algorithms for exploring disease-related brain dynamics. While theoretical work and electronic circuit testing succeeded, planned human studies in traumatic brain injury cases faced unforeseen collaboration obstacles and could not be completed as originally envisioned.

## Research Output

The five-year project generated:
- 19 peer-reviewed publications in venues including ICLR, NeurIPS, ICML, and PLoS Biology
- Four PhD dissertations spanning machine learning, statistical neuroscience, and computational modeling
- Novel insights bridging neuroscience and AI, examining how oscillatory dynamics in neural networks support computation

These findings are relevant to both biological understanding and artificial neural network design.

## Broader Impact

This work demonstrates how principled mathematical approaches to neural data can reveal fundamental computational principles, connecting questions in neuroscience with advances in machine learning and neurotechnology.
