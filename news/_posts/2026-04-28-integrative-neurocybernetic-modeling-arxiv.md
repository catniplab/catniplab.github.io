Our Perspective on the *cybernetic* future of large-scale model-centric integrative neuroscience is now on arXiv.
Predictive accuracy is not **understanding**: a Transformer-style foundation model that captures neural time series at scale does not, by itself, explain how behavior arises from the closed-loop coupling between brain, body, and environment.
We argue for redirecting the model class itself toward integrative neurocybernetic models -- closed-loop, embodied, dynamically structured, and organized around a controller pursuing latent objectives, with internal dynamics that correspond to biologically identifiable components.

The piece grew out of the [Neurocybernetics at Scale symposium](https://neurocybernetics.cc/neurocybernetics_at_scale_2025/) (Champalimaud Foundation, October 2025), and synthesizes a community effort around four desiderata -- understandability, neurocybernetic structure, structured variation across scales, and scalability -- and a program of complementary building blocks (nonlinear state-space models with meta-dynamical extensions, scalable inference, knowledge distillation, mixed open/closed-loop training, and connectomics-informed architectures).

(2026). Integrative Neurocybernetic Modeling in the Era of Large-Scale Neuroscience. [http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23903](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23903)
